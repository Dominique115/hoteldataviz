View 1 – Booking Timing and Pricing

This view looks at how early guests book (lead time) and how much they pay (ADR), and how this relationship changes across hotel type, customer type, and distribution channel.

Analytic question. Do guests who book earlier tend to pay more or less (ADR), and does this relationship differ by guest type or booking channel?

Key tasks:
  • Compare ADR across different lead times and hotel types.
  • Inspect how booking patterns differ across customer types and distribution channels.
  • Use brushing and clicking to focus on specific segments.

View Design

The view consists of four linked components:

  • A scatter plot of lead_time vs ADR with brush selection.
  • Trend lines (one per hotel type) summarizing the overall lead time–ADR pattern.
  • A stacked bar chart showing distribution channels within each customer type, filtered by the scatter selection.
  • A boxplot of lead time across customer types.

All components respond to user selections, and checkboxes for City Hotel and Resort Hotel act as global filters.

Channels and Encodings

The scatter plot uses position (lead time on the x-axis, ADR on the y-axis) as the primary channel to show the booking timing–pricing relationship. Color encodes hotel type and is used consistently across scatter points and trend lines. Opacity de-emphasizes points outside the current selection, guiding attention to active subsets.

The trend lines add a derived positional encoding that summarizes the general direction of the relationship for each hotel type. The stacked bar chart uses length to represent booking counts and color to encode distribution channels. The boxplot uses vertical position to compare lead time across customer types, showing variation, median, and spread.

Interaction

Two main interactions are implemented:

  • Brush selection on the scatter plot: dragging across the scatter filters the bar chart; brushed points keep full color and opacity while others fade.
  • Point selection on the stacked bar chart: clicking a bar segment (e.g., “Transient – Direct”) highlights the corresponding points in the scatter plot; other segments remain visible but semi-transparent, supporting multi-selection and comparison.

These interactions create a linked, bidirectional filtering system. Checkbox parameters for hotel type provide an additional global filter, updating the scatter, trend lines, bar chart, and boxplot simultaneously.

Critique

Overall, this view effectively links individual bookings with aggregated patterns. The scatter plot and trend lines help reveal how lead time relates to ADR across hotel types. The stacked bar chart supports understanding guest segmentation by channel, although category imbalances and some overplotting in the scatter can still make comparisons slightly challenging. The boxplot adds structural context for booking timing across customer types, even though its connection to ADR is indirect.
