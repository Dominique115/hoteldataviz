View 1 – Booking Timing and Pricing

This view looks at how early guests book (lead time) and how much they pay (ADR), and how this relationship differs across hotel type, customer type, and distribution channel.

Analytic question. Do guests who book earlier tend to pay more or less (ADR), and does this relationship differ by guest type or booking channel?

Key tasks:
  • Compare ADR across different lead times and hotel types.
  • See how booking patterns vary across customer types and channels.
  • Use brushing and clicking to focus on specific guest segments.

Design overview

This view combines four linked components to connect individual bookings with group patterns:

  • Scatter plot of lead time vs ADR with brush selection.
  • Trend lines (one per hotel type) showing overall timing–price trends.
  • Stacked bar chart of distribution channels within each customer type.
  • Boxplot of lead time across customer types.

Checkboxes for City Hotel and Resort Hotel act as a global filter across all four components.

Encodings

The scatter plot uses horizontal position for lead time and vertical position for ADR. Color indicates hotel type, and opacity fades non-selected points when brushing or clicking elsewhere in the view.

The bar chart uses bar length for booking counts and color for distribution channels. The boxplot uses vertical position to compare lead time distributions across customer types, showing median and spread.

Interactions

  • Brush on the scatter plot filters the bar chart to the selected bookings; brushed points stay fully opaque, others fade.
  • Click on bar segments (e.g., “Transient – Direct”) highlights the corresponding points in the scatter plot while keeping others semi-visible.
  • Hotel-type checkboxes toggle City vs. Resort hotels everywhere in the view.

Together, these create a two-way link between booking-level points and aggregated group views.

Critique

The view works well for seeing both detailed bookings and overall trends. The trend lines help clarify how lead time and ADR relate by hotel type, and the bar chart shows which segments drive most bookings.

Some overplotting remains in the scatter, and the bar chart can be hard to compare when one category dominates. The boxplot adds useful timing context but is only indirectly tied to ADR.
