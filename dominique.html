ADR is calculated by dividing sum of all lodging transactions by total number of staying nights.

View 2: How do customer type and market segments relate to different timing patterns across the booking span?

Customer Type:

  • Contract – Guests under corporate or negotiated-rate contracts.
  • Group – Bookings made for groups (e.g., events, conferences).
  • Transient – Individual, non-group travelers making independent bookings.
  • Transient-Party – Small parties or families traveling together but not large groups.

Market Segment:

  • Aviation Crew – Airline staff with dedicated negotiated contracts.
  • Complimentary (Free) – Free stays (e.g., service recovery or compensation).
  • Corporate Clients – Corporate-negotiated bookings.
  • Direct Booking – Booked directly through the hotel (website, phone).
  • Group Reservations – Reservations made under group codes.
  • Offline Tour Operators – Bookings through traditional travel agencies.
  • Online Travel Agents – Online booking platforms (website, apps).

Time stamps:

  • Days in Waitlist – Time between making request to book until successful booking.
  • Days before Arrival – Time betweeen successful booking to arrival at the hotel.
  • Days Stayed – Time between arrival at hotel to departure.
