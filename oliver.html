Oliver Huang – Booking Timing, Pricing, and Cancellation Risk

Theme: Booking timing, pricing patterns, and cancellation risk
This set of views explores how when guests book, how much they pay (ADR), and how they commit to payment relate to the likelihood that their bookings are cancelled.

Overall Research Question

How do booking timing and pricing patterns relate to cancellation risk across hotel and customer segments?

View-specific Analytic Questions

  • View 1: Do guests who book earlier tend to pay more or less (ADR), and does this relationship differ by guest type or booking channel?
  • View 2: How do payment commitments and customer characteristics interact to influence cancellation likelihood?

Views Overview

View 1 – Booking Timing and Pricing

A linked multi-view display that connects individual bookings (scatter + trend lines) with distributions across customer types and channels (stacked bar chart and boxplot). It focuses on how lead time relates to ADR across hotel types and guest segments.

  • Compare ADR across different lead times.
  • Contrast booking patterns between City vs. Resort hotels.
  • Drill down into customer types and distribution channels.

View 2 – Payment Commitments & Cancellation Risk

A histogram + line chart combined with a heatmap that show how ADR levels, deposit types, and customer segments relate to cancellation rates. ADR sliders and hotel selection allow focused exploration across price ranges.

  • Compare cancellation risk across deposit and customer types.
  • See how risk changes over ADR ranges.
  • Contrast patterns between City and Resort hotels.
